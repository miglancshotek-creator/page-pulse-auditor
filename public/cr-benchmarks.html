Revenue Leak Formula
Benchmark CR = MAX(table value, current CR + 1%)
Gap = (Benchmark CR − Current CR) ÷ 100
Monthly leak = Gap × Visitors × AOV
The +1% floor rule ensures the leak is always calculated as a realistic improvement — even if the client's CR already exceeds the industry average. A 1 percentage point gain is a conservative, defensible target for any page with CRO issues.

Example: 3,000 visitors · 1.8% CR · Google Search · E-commerce · €65 AOV
Benchmark = 3.2% → Gap = 1.4% → Leak = 3,000 × 0.014 × 65 = €2,730/month

Usage Notes

Which benchmark to use

Always use the cell that matches both the business type row and the traffic source column. If a client runs Google Search Ads for their e-commerce store, the benchmark is 3.2% — not the generic e-commerce average.

The +1% floor rule

If the client's current CR is already above the benchmark (e.g. 4.5% vs 3.2%), use current CR + 1.0pp as the target instead. This ensures the calculation always shows a realistic improvement opportunity.

Lead gen vs e-commerce AOV

For lead generation and service businesses, AOV = estimated value of one qualified lead — typically the average contract value divided by the close rate. Ask the client: "What is one new customer worth to you?"

Cold outreach workflow

When you don't have the client's real data: use Similarweb for visitor estimate, assume their CR is 1.5% (conservative), use the benchmark from this table, and ask only for AOV in your follow-up email to recalculate with real numbers.
